Loop Engineering: The Next Big Shift in How We Work With AI A new idea is trending in AI right now, and it is called "loop engineering." For the last two years, we worked with AI agents one prompt at a time: you type, you wait, you read the answer, you type again. Loop engineering changes this. Instead of prompting the agent yourself again and again, you design a system a "loop". The agent takes an action, checks its own result, fixes its mistakes, and keeps going until the goal is truly met. It became popular in June 2026 after engineers at Google and Anthropic shared the same simple message: stop prompting by hand, start designing loops. Why should you know this? Because the most valuable skill is moving one level up. Writing a good prompt still matters, but the real power now is in designing the loop around the agent, the goal, the checks, the stop conditions, and the memory. A good loop can multiply the output of one strong engineer; a bad loop can multiply a bad decision just as fast. This is quickly becoming how the best teams already build with AI, so whether you are a developer, a product manager, or a leader, it is worth understanding now.
