Feed post Sagar Mainkar • 3rd+ Co-founder , Catomz | AI + Cybersecurity | Ex-Qualys, SAS | 20+ Yrs Building Products at Scale 5h • Follow Is Loop Engineering the new meta in agentic AI? Recently, Peter Steinberger (creator of the viral OpenClaw agent, now at OpenAI) and Boris Cherny (lead of Claude Code at Anthropic) both made waves with the same message: the real power comes when you stop simply prompting agents and start designing loops for them. The idea is elegant. Instead of a single prompt, a loop is a repeatable cycle: Goal → Context → Action → Verify → Continue/Stop The agent iterates autonomously until a verifiable goal is met. When it works — especially with tool use or via Model Context Protocol (MCP), it genuinely feels like a step-change in what software can do. But there is a structural cost most teams are not accounting for. Every iteration carries the full weight of the expanding context window. A simple autonomous debugging or triage loop can burn thousands of tokens in minutes. This leads to an uncomfortable question worth asking- the loudest voices championing loop-first architectures happen to work at the largest inference providers. I am not suggesting anything , but it is just a structural incentive worth being aware of when you are designing your own systems. If you are building with agent loops, a few constraints are worth baking in from day one: 1️⃣ Bound the budget: Set hard stop conditions and strict iteration limits on your loops. 2️⃣ Maintain control: Implement human-in-the-loop validation for high-stakes actions. 3️⃣ Architect for cost: Treat your token usage as a core architectural constraint, not an afterthought. Autonomy is the future, but do not let a poorly constrained loop burn a hole in your cloud budget. What are your thoughts?
