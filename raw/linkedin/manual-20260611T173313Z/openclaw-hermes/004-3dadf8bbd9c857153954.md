I wanted to create a KYC and Engagement onboarding platform for my law firm with open source and a privacy and security first approach. With the help of my agents Dolores (Hermes, GPT 5.5) and Donna (OpenClaw, Claude Opus and recently Fable) I designed and installed two stacks using OpnForm for interactive KYC forms and DocuSeal for execution of the engagement letter. A prospective client receives an email with a secure link to the correct KYC form in the applicable language and a secure link to directly upload sensitive KYC documents (which are immediately and automatically encrypted) to my cloud provider. Separately, the client receives an SMS with the code required to open the KYC link. Once the KYC side has been completed by the client I receive an email. I do the required required reviews etc and subquently, the client receives an engagement letter through a secure link. In this entire process no third party is involved other than my European zero-knowledge end-to-end encryption cloud provider. I am a strong believer in privacy and security and I recommend every law firm to review their procedures and SAAS providers and consider replacing them with open source solutions in combination with privacy and security first cloud providers. Side note one: The system was initially designed by GPT 5.5 and Opus 4.8 and installed by GPT 5.5. When Fable was introduced I had it review the stacks and procedures. I was blown away by the intelligence of Fable and the improvements (especially hardening) it came up with.
